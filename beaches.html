Where should you take your next swim?

Just like personalities, it’s safe to say that no two beaches are the same. Are you an energetic jogger? A lazy sunbather? A party-loving beach bum? Well, whatever your vibe, there’s a beach out there with your name written in the sand. So shower yourself in sunscreen and slap on your best speedos or bathing suit – we’re going to find your perfect summer seaside haven!

Beach Map!
Sandhammaren

The silky white sands of Sandhammaren match the best beaches of the Mediterranean. It’s been voted as Sweden’s best beach several times. Laze around around on white, powdery sand, take a dip in the sea, or enjoy a beach walk that goes for miles. The location of the beach are conducive to some pumping ocean breaks drawing surfers from all over the globe. In need for a breathtaking getaway? This is the place to be!

It’s the perfect place for sipping cocktails, bathing on hot summer days, or just walking along the dunes and inland pine forests in any season. Sandhammaren is at the southernmost point of Sweden, so currents can be dangerous. Be careful when you swim here – maybe walk out and swim back? Parking spaces can be hard to find if you come on the busiest days in the summer. After sunbathing and swimming, then take a walk through the nature reserve that has a wealth of wildlife, as well as Sweden’s most southerly elk population! Just north of the beach.

Åhus

Åhus is Skåne's east coast's main destination. Åhus beach probably has the finest and whitest sand across Sweden. The powder-like sand beaches are a treat for those looking for a less crowded alternative. The beach stretches from Kristianstad to Österlen. The long, sandy beaches is close to the medieval town centre Åhus, a well-preserved idyll and is the perfect summer destination for family vacation. Stroll through the narrow, meandering streets and square with traditions back to the Middle Ages.

Åhus is the home of world-famous Absolut Vodka, take a tour and taste a sample of this delicious, trendy drink at The Absolut factory. Åhus beach is located at the "Eel coast" next bo big tobacco fields. Summer in Åhus is filled with sporting and entertainment events, such as Åhus Beach Handball and Åhus Beach Football, the Eel Festival, wine walk, harbor party, jazz festival and more. As you can you can read Åhus offers everything from Absolut Vodka to Eel. Cheers!

Västra Hamnen

Take a dip next to Turning Torso in Västra Hamnen = Western Harbour in Malmö. Turning Torso is 190 metres high and is the tallest building in Scandinavia. Designed by Spanish architect Santiago Calatrava, this awesome building twists its way into the skyline, resembling the shape of a human being. Västra Hamnen is only ten minutes from the Central station and is close to the town centre.

Vibrant, bayside Västra Hamnen represents the modern face of Malmö. It's a popular spot to stroll, sunbathing, diving from the piers or just sip coffee, but mostly people come to be amazed at the view of the Öresund bridge.

Skanör Falsterbo

The south coast of Skåne has a huge range of contenders in the best beach category and one of them are Skanör and Falsterbo. Two medieval towns joined together on the elongated reef-like western part of the Falsterbo Peninsula. Casually known as the Swedish Riviera. The small bathing huts along the white sand dunes in the Höllviken, Falsterbo and Skanör coastline are beautifully painted in different colours. The sand is white and extremely fine. The water is crystal clear and clean, with a smooth undulating sandy bottom. Perfect for families and small children, this long, shallow, sandy beach is great for just splashing around, Once you have had a refreshing swim you can effortlessly move to one of the eateries for a delicious meal.

Skanör Falsterbo is a popular summer hang-out and party spot, with lots of open-air bars, jet skis, motorboats and Malmö weekenders.

Mölle och Kullaberg

Northwest Skåne is a place of steep cliffs, spectacular caves, waves that whip off surfboards, and landscapes perfect for hiking. Despite its distance from most of the cities on the East Coast most Swedes will agree with you that this is one coastal hotspot that you shouldn't miss out on visiting.The isolated location of the beach only adds to its untouched beauty with rocky headlands and untamed wilderness making Kullaberg Mölle an impressive stretch of sand completely different to any other beach on the list. Get an exclusive access to some of the most secluded beaches in Skåne, with amazing diving and snorkelling spots close to shore. The picturesque little fishing village of Mölle developed into one of Sweden's first seaside resorts in the1890s. Mölle became known as a den of sin, as women and men bathed together, something that was unthinkable at that time

This area of northwest Skåne offers high waves and superb surfing. The waves hit hard against the rock walls, turning the whole area into a clean surfing paradise on windy days, even in the middle of summer. The area attracts surfers from all over northern Europe and beyond. When you’re not surfing you can climb the stunning cliffs of Kullaberg.

Stenshuvud

Stenshuvud (Stone Head) is Sweden's southernmost national park on the east coast of Skåne and offers delightful hiking trails viewpoints over the sea, a white sandy beach and crystal clear waters. The shore is very shallow and you have to walk quite far into the sea to swim. Billed as Skåne’s most exotic beach, it’s a magnet in summer. The fine sand and shallow sea make it a paradise for families. But be careful of the currents when you swim further out.

Enjoy beautiful sunset strolls and climb the 97 metre-high viewpoint over the sea at the national park. After a tough hike, pop down to the shimmering sandy beach in the park, take a dop to cool off and watch magical sunrises over the sea. Next to the beach you can see cows, horses and cheep walking around. Mañana, mañana...

Knäbäckshusen

What makes Knäbäckshusens Strand so popular and one of the most beautiful beaches in the southern Swedish province of Skåne is its special location and atmosphere. It is situated at the foot of a high dune belt, which is forested with dense pine trees all the way down to the beach. This creates a feeling of seclusion as well as an almost exotic flair due to the trees that grow right down to the beach and partly reach out to the water. The range of activities including snorkelling, kayaking, wildlife spotting and hiking make it a well rounded beach appealing to a variety of people. Knäbäckshusens Strand consists of almost white fine-grained sand and stretches for more than three kilometres from the fishing village of Vik in the south to the border of Stenshuvuds Nationalpark in the north, where the beach line merges seamlessly into Stenshuvuds Strand. The golden sands and pristine waters make it an ideal swimming spot for families.

Knäbäckshusen is the standout among the many breathtaking beaches of Skåne. First time visitors are regularly speechless when they try to describe its beauty and it's not hard to see why. Knäbäckshusen beach is famous for its untouched stretch of shoreline and lapping turquoise waters. It gives you a wonderful Mediterranean feel.

Ribban

Malmö’s answer to Copa Cabana, Ribersborg beach (called Ribban by locals). Ribban beach is where the young, the old, the bold and the beautiful of Malmö and visitors from around the world enjoy swimming, sunbathing, or just generally lazing around in the summer months. In fact, on any given day of the year Ribban beach is busy with families and swimmers enjoying the simple pleasures of a day at the beach. The beach's popularity is owed to the fact it is easily accessible from the city. Skåne’s capital is all about culture, exciting cuisine, innovative architecture and a vibrant social scene. Spoil yourself in an area that has more pubs and restaurants per capita than anywhere else in Sweden.

Relax on the long sandy beach, bathe in shallow, child-friendly water, walk the long piers overlooking Copenhagen (just 15 minutes away by train), or pop into northern Europe’s largest cold bath house Kallis.
